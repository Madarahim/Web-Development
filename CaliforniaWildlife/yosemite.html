Yosemite National Park

Not just a great valley, but a shrine to human foresight, the strength of granite, the power of glaciers, the persistence of life, and the tranquility of the High Sierra. First protected in 1864, Yosemite National Park is best known for its waterfalls, but within its nearly 1,200 square miles, you can find deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, a vast wilderness area, and much more.

Explore California provides both jeeps and large buses, for large groups of people.

Wildlife

Yosemite supports more than 400 species of vertebrates, including fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals. Insects abound as well, with the recent discovery of two species not believed to exist anywhere else in the world. The high diversity of animal species coincides with the striking elevational gradient and topographic variability of the park.

Keep your eyes peeled during the Safari. You might see something incredible!

Education

Teaching others, especially the youth, has been Yosemite's goal since it was established as a National Park.

What is special about Yosemite?

Whether its the incredible rock formations carved from glaciers or its lush fields of flowers, Yosemite transcends beauty.
