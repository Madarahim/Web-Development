Los Padres National Forest

Los Padres National Forest encompasses approximately 1.75 million acres of central California's scenic Coast and Transverse Ranges. The forest stretches across almost 220 miles from north to south and consists of two separate land divisions. The northern division is within Monterey County and northern San Luis Obispo County and includes the beautiful Big Sur Coast and scenic interior areas. The "main division" of the forest includes lands within San Luis Obispo, Santa Barbara, Ventura and Kern Counties.

Wildlife Sanctuary

Los Padres is the strongest bastion of hope for the Endangered California Condor. The species, slowyl but surely, been increasing with Los Padres being a the best place to see them in their natural habitat.

The forest also contains other wildlife like Hawks, Bobcats, Black Bears, Mountain Lions, RattleSnakes, and much more!

Terrain

Transverse Ranges, and its largest peak Mount Pinos, is beloved by mountain climbers and casual hikers alike.

What is special about Los Padres?

Whether its the Cliff-Side Beach of Big Sur or the Oak Savannahs spread across the park, Los Padres is diversity exemplified.
